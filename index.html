Traditional Boil

Traditional Crawfish Boil

Single pot and 1 30-40lb sack of crawfish (Serves 8-12)

Boiling crawfish requires approximately 2 quarts of water per 1 pound of crawfish. For example, 30 pounds of crawfish takes 15 gallons of water.

A more precise method of determining the amount of water needed in the pot is to place all of the fruit/vegetables in the bottom of empty pot (outside of basket to keep everything seperated). Put CLEAN crawfish in basket on top of veggies. Fill with water until the top of the water is 2-3 inches above crawfish. Remove everything but the water from the pot and mark the water fill line on a paddle or on the inside of the pot. Marking both is always a good idea.

Note: The process below takes approximately 1½ hours from the time you fire up the jet burner to serving crawfish.

Equipment

  • 1 80qt pot (or larger) with basket
  • 1 Propane jet burner with regulator (the higher the BTU, the better)
  • 1 Full bottle of propane (second backup bottle of propane is also recomended)
  • 1 Paddle
  • 1 Cooler to hold the cooked crawfish
  • 1 10lb bag of ice
  • Heat resistant gloves

Seasoning

  • 4 lbs Frog Bone Cajun Seasoning (1¼ lbs per 10 lbs of crawfish)
  • 16 oz cayenne pepper (32 oz for extra spicy)
  • 20 bay leaves
  • 1 cup garlic powder (8oz)
  • 1 cup onion powder (8oz)
  • 2 bags Zatarain's crab boil
  • 12 oz Lawry's Coarse Ground Garlic Salt
  • 16 oz Zatarans liquid crab boil (32 oz for extra spicy)
  • 1 bottle Louisiana Hot Sauce (6oz)
  • 1 bottle Zatarain's Garlic & Onion (8oz)
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 8-12 Lemons (3 per 10 lbs of crawfish)
  • 4-6 large oranges (1½ per 10 lbs of crawfish)
  • 5 lbs yellow onions
  • 1 head of celery
  • 8-12 bulbs of garlic(3 per 10 lbs of crawfish)

Meats/Vegetables

  • 4 lbs of Conecuh sausage (⅓ lb per person)
  • 12 Potatoes (1 per person)
  • 24oz (4 packages) baby bella mushrooms (2 oz per person)
  • 12 frozen precooked corn on the cob (1 per person)

Step 1: Wash and Purge Crawfish

  • Click here for detailed instructions

Step 2: Prep

  • Combine all dry seasonings into a single container
  • Combine all liquid seasonings into a single container
  • Cut all lemons in half
  • Cut all oranges in half
  • Cut all garlic in half
  • Puree onions and add to liquid seasonings
  • Puree celery and add to liquid seasonings
  • Cut all garlic bulbs in half horizontally and add to liquid seasonings
  • Cut all sausage into 2-3 in segments

Step 3: Setup

  • Connect propane to jet burner and test
  • Place basket in pot
  • Fill pot with 12-15 gallons of water
  • Light jet burner and turn regulator to max output

Step 4: Add Seasonings

  • Add all dry seasonings to the pot
  • Add all liquid seasonings to the pot
  • Squeeze lemons and organges into the pot dropping the peels in the pot as well
  • Place lid on pot
Once the water reaches a rolling boil (approximately 25-30 minutes), continue to boil for another 10 minutes.

Step 5: Add Potatos

  • Reduce burner heat enough to maintain a rolling boil
  • Place lid on pot
  • Boil for 7-10 minutes (10+ minutes for med-large potatos cut in half)

Step 6: Add Sausage, Mushrooms, and Fresh Vegetables

  • Add Sausage
  • Add Mushrooms
  • Place lid on pot
  • Boil for 5 minutes

Step 7: Crawfish Time!!!

  • Increase burner output to maximum
  • Remove steaming basket from the pot
  • Dump crawfish into the basket
  • Carefully lower the basket in the pot
  • Place lid on pot
  • Return to a boil (10-15 minutes depending on the burner BTU output)
When you see the first signs of boiling, typically steam billowing out from under the lid, continue to boil for another 3 minutes.

Step 8: Shutoff Burner & Cool Pot

  • Crawfish should be bright red and floating
  • Turn off burner
  • Add frozen corn and push below crawfish
  • Add a 10 lb bag of ice and mix thoroughly (stops the cooking process)
  • The ideal temperature range to soak the crawfish is 150-160 degrees
  • Crawfish should start sinking after a few minutes
  • Do not put the lid back on after this point

Step 9: Soak to Taste & Serve

  • Soak for 20-30 minutes (taste test at 15)
  • Most of the crawfish should be completely sumberged
  • Longer soak times = spicier crawfish!
Steaming

Steamed Crawfish

The Unconventional Approach

Multiple pots are ideal but not necessary for steaming crawfish. A large cooler can be used as a continer for the soak. Just make sure that you have a large strainer to remove the crawfish after they're done soaking. The ingredients below are good for up to 200lbs of crawfish. You will definitely want to puree your onions and celery. Also needed is a thermometer.

STEP 1: WASH/PURGE THE CRAWFISH
Dry Ingredients:
6lbs (4lb bag + 2lb bag) Frog Bone Cajun Seasoning (12 cups)
2 cups cayenne pepper (16oz)
20 bay leaves
2 cups garlic powder (8oz)
2 bags Zatarain's crab boil
1/2 cup Hondashi
Liquid Ingredients:
3 cups Zatarans crab boil (8oz)
1 bottle Louisiana Hot Sauce (6oz)
2 bottles Zatarain's Garlic & Onion (8oz)
1 cup white vinegar
Seasoning Fruits/Vegetables:
32 oz Lemon Juice
16 oz Orange Juice
5 lbs pureed yellow onions
1 head of pureed celery
Meats/Vegetables:
4 Packages of Conecuh sausage
12 Potatoes
24oz (4 packages) baby bella mushrooms
12 frozen precooked corn on the cob (cut in half)
Prep work:
Combine all seasoning fruit/vegetables
Combine all dry ingredients
Combine all pureed ingredients
Combine all liquid ingredients
Cut up sausage
Add water to pot and set burner to HIGH:
Add all liquid seasonings
Add all seasoning fruits/vegetables
Add pureed onionis/celery
Place lid on pot
*Approximately 25-30 minutes to start boiling
Once water reaches a rolling boil:
Add all dry ingredients
Place lid on pot
Boil for 10 minutes with lid on:
Add potatoes
Reduce heat enough to maintain a rolling boil
Place lid on pot
Boil for another 10 minutes:
Add Conecuh sausage
Add mushrooms
Place lid on pot
Boil for 5 minutes:
Turn off propane
Add FROZEN corn
Spray the outside of the pot with cold water the until the soak reaches a temperature of 165 degrees
Once the soak reaches a temperature of 165 degrees:
Stop spraying the outside of the pot
Let the meats/veggies soak for 20-30 miutes to taste
When the soaking process is complete:
Dump the meats/veggies in a cooler to keep warm
Remove the pot from the burner-this will now be the soaking pot
Put approximately 2 inches of water in the second pot-this will be the steaming pot
Turn burner on HIGH until the water reaches a rolling boil
Once a rolling boil is acheived:
Place the crawfish in the pot
Put lid on pot
Leave burner on HIGH
Once a foam covers all of the crawfish:
Let the crawfish continue to steam for 4-1/2 minutes
After 4-1/2 minutes have passed:
Shut off burner
Move the crawfish to the soaker pot
Soak for 20-30 minutes (taste test at 15)
Repeat the steaming process as needed for up to 200lbs of crawfish

Lessons

Lessons Learned

...the Hard Way

  • Expect approximately 5-10% of your crawfish to die. This is normal when they have to be transported long distances.
  • Don't submerge them in water that isn't circulating for longer than a few minutes. Though crawfish won't drown in sufficiently oxygenated water, they will suffocate from a lack of oxygen in the water.
  • They will die if their gills get too dry. Its best to cover them with ice and an extremely wet towell when they're not in water.
  • Salt isn't necessary for purging. Fresh water from the tap or hose will do the trick.
  • Have a minimum of 2 propane tanks available for every crawfish boil.
  • Don't dispose of crawfish heads indoors. After everyone is done eating, bag up and dispose of them as soon as possible. Don't forget about the DOA crawfish that were removed during the washing/purging process. Those suckers smell BAD; especially after they've sat around for a day or two.
  • The soak that gives the crawfish they're incredible flavor can kill your grass if you empty your pot while the water is still hot.
